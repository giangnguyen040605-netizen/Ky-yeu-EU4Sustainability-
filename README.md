# EXPLORING ENABLERS AND INHIBITORS OF ELECTRIC VEHICLE ADOPTION INTENTION IN VIETNAM: A DUAL-FACTOR THEORY PERSPECTIVE

Research paper accepted for publication in the EU4Sustainability Conference Proceedings.  
Temporary upload before the official publication link is released by the publisher.

## Research Overview
This research examines the factors influencing electric vehicle (EV) adoption intention in Vietnam through the perspective of Dual-Factor Theory, focusing on both enabling and inhibiting factors affecting consumer behavior and sustainable transportation adoption.

The study investigates how environmental benefits, policy incentives, driving enjoyment, financial barriers, infrastructure limitations, and technological concerns influence consumers’ intention to adopt electric vehicles in an emerging market context.

## Methodology
- Conducted literature review and focus group discussions to refine measurement scales
- Collected survey data from 460 respondents aged 18–45
- Applied quantitative data analysis using SmartPLS 3
- Performed reliability testing, factor analysis, and structural equation modeling (SEM)

## Key Findings
- Environmental benefits, policy incentives, and driving enjoyment positively influence EV adoption intention
- Infrastructure and technological barriers significantly hinder adoption decisions
- Financial barriers showed weaker influence due to evolving market conditions and financing accessibility
- Enablers and inhibitors simultaneously affect consumer decision-making, supporting the Dual-Factor Theory framework

## Research Contribution
This study contributes to the understanding of electric vehicle adoption behavior in emerging markets while providing practical implications for policymakers and businesses promoting sustainable transportation solutions.
